Privacy Policy

Last updated: 6 July 2026

Rogue Miner: Void Frontier respects your privacy. Your core game progress is saved locally on your device. The game does not ask for your name, email address or any other personal information.

To power the shared galaxy and asynchronous multiplayer, the game uses the following services operated by Google (Firebase), which process data in accordance with their own privacy policies:

  • Firebase Authentication (anonymous) — when the game first connects, it is assigned a random anonymous identifier. This identifier is not linked to your identity and is used only to tell player profiles apart.
  • Cloud Firestore — stores gameplay data associated with that anonymous identifier: the pilot name you choose in-game, your faction, conquered star systems, base status, asynchronous raids and leaderboard statistics. This data is transmitted over encrypted connections (HTTPS).

See Google's Privacy Policy for details on how Google processes data.

The game contains no advertising and does not sell or share your data with third parties for marketing purposes. The game does not knowingly collect data from children under the age of consent.

Data deletion: to request deletion of the multiplayer data tied to your anonymous identifier, contact us at the address below and include your in-game pilot name. Uninstalling the game removes all locally stored progress.
